About me

My name is Yitbarek Alemu, and I am a software engineering student at Addis Ababa University. I was born and raised in Hossana, a city in the southern region of Ethiopia. Hossana is known for its landsape and incredible weather.

Education

I completed all my pre-university years at Holy Trinity Primary and Secondary School, an exceptional institution known for its vibrant community. This school emphasized personal growth, instilling confidence and a sense of purpose in its students. Its nurturing environment encouraged creativity, critical thinking, and resilience, providing a foundation not just for academics but for life skills. Through dedicated teachers, diverse extracurricular activities, and a supportive peer network, Holy Trinity became more than just a school—it was a transformative chapter in my journey to becoming my best self.

Hobbies

Mostly, I read to explore diverse worlds and ideas, gaining new perspectives and insights. My reading habits vary widely—from immersing myself in fiction that expands my imagination to diving into non-fiction that enriches my understanding of history, science, and human behavior. Books are my constant companions, offering both an escape and a means of growth. They challenge me to think critically, spark creativity, and connect with experiences beyond my own. Reading, for me, is both a passion and a tool for lifelong learning.
