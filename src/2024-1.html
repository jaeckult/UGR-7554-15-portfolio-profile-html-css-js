The Oddity of Existence

A reflection on the peculiar nature of being and how it shapes us.


Existence, a strange and wondrous thing,
Not just to live, but know that life takes wing—
An observer, watching life's unfolding art,
A quiet thought that lingers in the heart.

Beneath the stars, a blanket dark and deep,
Ancient lights their million-year-old sleep,
A distant yet an intimate embrace,
My tiny place, within this boundless space.

Awe and absurdity, a curious blend,
Stardust and reactions, without end,
To ponder cosmos, fragile and so grand,
A fleeting touch, upon this shifting sand.

Mundane routines, a daily, weary chase,
Yet curiosity, a boundless, endless space,
Why are we here? What meaning can we find?
The questions call, and leave no peace behind.

Not answers sought, but being's odd embrace,
Moments of weight, in time and fleeting space,
Beauty in mystery, chaos' gentle hand,
Just to exist, and softly understand.
